Chapter 17 · Day 23 In‑Class Notes

Governmental Entities
& Fund Accounting

A structured walkthrough of how state and local governments account for public money — from GASB's mandate through funds, budgets, and the journal entries that keep a city's books straight.

Unlike private businesses, governments hold resources on behalf of the public — and the public has a right to know how those resources are used. Accountability is not just an accounting principle here; it is the entire purpose of governmental financial reporting.

Every fund, budget entry, and journal entry in this chapter is ultimately a tool for demonstrating that public money was collected and spent in accordance with the law and the public's trust.

ACC 200 Note

This material covers governmental accounting at an introductory ACC 200 level. Government accounting is a broad and complex field — state and local governments engage in many activities, transactions, and reporting requirements that we will not address in this course. The goal here is to build a working foundation in the concepts and journal entries most relevant to this level, which is also consistent with what is tested on the CPA exam.
